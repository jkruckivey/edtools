### Element 6A: Practice Quiz - Betting Economics Check

**Question 1: Handle vs. Revenue**

A state reports $5 billion in annual sports betting handle. If operators average 8% hold percentage, what is their approximate gross revenue?

A) $5 billion
B) $800 million
C) $400 million ‚úÖ
D) $40 million

**Feedback (Correct):** Correct! Gross revenue = Handle x Hold %. $5B x 8% = $400M. Handle is total wagered; revenue is the operator's margin. This distinction is critical‚Äîdon't confuse market size (handle) with operator economics (revenue).

**Feedback (Incorrect):** Remember: Handle = total amount wagered. Hold % = operator margin. Revenue = Handle x Hold %. If $5B is wagered and operators keep 8%, their revenue is...?

---

**Question 2: Customer Acquisition Economics**

FanDuel spends $400 to acquire a new customer. If that customer's lifetime value is $1,600, what's the return on customer acquisition?

A) 25%
B) 100%
C) 300% ‚úÖ
D) 400%

**Feedback (Correct):** Correct! ROI = (LTV - CAC) / CAC = ($1,600 - $400) / $400 = 300%. This economics justifies aggressive sponsorship spending‚Äîeach customer acquired through sponsorships generates $1,200 in profit.

**Feedback (Incorrect):** Calculate ROI: (Lifetime Value - Acquisition Cost) / Acquisition Cost. If you invest $400 and get $1,600 back, your return is...?

## Closed-Loop Attribution: Betting's Secret Weapon

In Module 2, you learned why most sponsorships measure Reach well, Engagement moderately, and Conversion poorly. Betting partnerships solve this.

**Traditional sponsor attribution:**
Fan sees Nike logo on jersey ‚Üí ??? ‚Üí Buys Nike shoes at Foot Locker 3 weeks later
**Attribution confidence: 10-20%** (no direct link between exposure and purchase)

**Betting operator attribution:**
Fan sees FanDuel ad during game ‚Üí Scans QR code ‚Üí Downloads app ‚Üí Creates account (promo code tracked) ‚Üí Deposits $50 ‚Üí Places first bet ‚Üí Becomes recurring bettor
**Attribution confidence: 95%+** (every step tracked with user-level data)

This closed-loop measurement is why FanDuel pays the NFL $1B+ while Nike pays $138M. FanDuel can prove their ROI; Nike estimates theirs. When you can prove ROI, you pay premiums. When you estimate ROI, you negotiate discounts.

**For your activation plan:** Consider how betting integration could enable attribution for traditional sponsors. If a beer brand partners with FanDuel on a promotion, they suddenly have access to closed-loop data they couldn't get alone.
