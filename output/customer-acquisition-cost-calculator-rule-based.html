Understanding the Metrics

CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost): Total marketing and sales expenses divided by the number of new customers acquired. Lower is better, but must be balanced with growth goals.

LTV (Lifetime Value): The total revenue a business can expect from a single customer over their entire relationship. Calculated as: Average Order Value × Purchase Frequency × Customer Lifespan.

LTV:CAC Ratio: A key metric for business sustainability. Ratios above 3:1 are generally considered healthy, while ratios below 1:1 indicate unprofitable customer acquisition.

Payback Period: The time required to recover the cost of acquiring a customer. Shorter payback periods improve cash flow and reduce risk.
