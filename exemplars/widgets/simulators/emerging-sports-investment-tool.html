Emerging Sports Investment Tool

Explore the economics of investing in emerging sports properties

What This Tool Does
  • Models investment returns for emerging sports properties (NWSL, WNBA, pickleball, women's rugby, European women's soccer)
  • Calculates IRR, Sharpe ratio, and break-even timelines based on your assumptions
  • Compares your scenario against S&P 500 and established sports benchmarks
  • Shows sensitivity analysis for best/worst case scenarios
Model Simplifications
  • Entry costs and valuations are estimates: NWSL at $40M, WNBA at $50M are approximations from public reporting — actual transaction prices vary significantly
  • Growth rates are highly uncertain: 20-40% annual growth for emerging sports is plausible but not guaranteed — many leagues fail or grow slowly
  • Exit multiples are speculative: The model assumes you can sell at a multiple — liquidity in sports ownership is limited
  • Risk premiums are subjective: The 8-15% risk premiums are illustrative — actual risk depends on league governance, media deals, and market conditions
  • Benchmarks are simplified: S&P 500 at 10% and established sports at 12% are long-term averages, not guarantees

This tool illustrates how assumptions shape investment outcomes. Emerging sports are high-risk, illiquid investments. Use this to build intuition, not to make actual investment decisions.
