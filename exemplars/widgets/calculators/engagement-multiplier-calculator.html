The Bettor Engagement Premium

Why a small segment of betting fans drives outsized viewership value

The Core Insight
Bettors watch 30-50% more games than non-bettors
Why: Financial stakes increase attention and commitment
Effect: Small % of fans → disproportionate viewership share

The size of this premium is debated and varies widely across studies.

Why this matters for media rights: Rights holders value bettor-heavy audiences because each bettor viewer generates more total viewership than a non-bettor. This helps explain why betting partnerships command premium prices — they help attract and retain the highest-engagement segment. It is also possible that highly engaged fans are more likely to become bettors, rather than betting causing engagement.

Key Takeaway

The engagement premium helps explain one reason why betting has become strategically valuable in media rights negotiations. A small percentage of betting fans generates disproportionate viewership, making sports content more valuable to broadcasters and streamers. However, the precise magnitude of this effect varies by sport, market, and measurement method. While commercially attractive, betting-driven engagement also raises regulatory and ethical questions that are not captured in this model.
