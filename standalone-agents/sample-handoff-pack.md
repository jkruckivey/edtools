# Sample 1: Self-Paced with Interactive Simulator

```
HANDOFF PACK

Course format: Self-paced

Module:
  Number: 1
  Title: Introduction to Marketing Analytics

Outcomes practiced:
  - MLO 1: Explain the role of analytics in modern marketing decision-making
  - MLO 2: Identify common marketing analytics metrics and what they measure
  - MLO 3: Interpret basic campaign performance reports
  - MLO 4: Distinguish between vanity metrics and actionable metrics
  - MLO 5: Recommend simple optimization actions based on campaign data

Element sequence:
  1. Hook / Bridge-in (Text)
  2. Learning Outcomes Widget
  3. Teaching Block 1 (MLO 1)
  4. Practice Quiz 1 (MLO 1) — 1 question
  5. Teaching Block 2 (MLO 2)
  6. Tiles: Common Marketing Metrics (MLO 2)
  7. AI Chat Widget: Marketing Analytics Expert
  8. Teaching Block 3 (MLO 3)
  9. Interactive Widget: Campaign Report Explorer (MLO 3)
  10. Source Accordion: Industry Benchmarks
  11. Teaching Block 4 (MLO 4)
  12. Practice Quiz 2 (MLO 4) — 2 questions
  13. Teaching Block 5 (MLO 5)
  14. Practice Activity: Optimization Recommendations (MLO 5)
  15. Go Deeper Accordion: Marketing Analytics Resources
  16. Module Reflection
  17. Final Assessment Connection
  18. Transition Note

Widget specifications:
  Campaign Report Explorer:
    Type: Interactive Simulator
    Purpose: Learners adjust campaign parameters and see how metrics change
    Inputs: Budget allocation (%), channel selection, time period
    Outputs: CTR, CPA, ROAS, conversion rate (calculated)
    Outcome: MLO 3
    Status: TO BE BUILT

AI Chat Expert:
  Name: Marketing Analytics Expert
  Focus areas:
    - How to read and interpret campaign dashboards
    - Difference between correlation and causation in marketing data
    - When to trust metrics vs when to dig deeper
  Common questions:
    - "What's a good CTR for my industry?"
    - "How do I know if my campaign is actually working?"
    - "Should I focus on reach or engagement?"
  Boundaries: Redirect technical implementation questions (SQL, APIs) to technical resources

Constraints:
  - Time target: 45 minutes
  - Special requirements: None

Content notes:
  - Focus on digital marketing context (social, search, email campaigns)
  - Use realistic but fictional campaign data for examples
  - Target audience: Marketing professionals new to analytics
  - Tone: Practical and grounded, avoid jargon without explanation
```

# Sample 2: Cohort with Case Study Infographic

```
HANDOFF PACK

Course format: Cohort

Module:
  Number: 1 (Week 1, Module 2)
  Title: Understanding Your Customer Base

Outcomes practiced:
  - WLO 1.1: Identify key customer segmentation variables
  - WLO 1.2: Analyze customer data to create persona profiles
  - WLO 1.3: Apply segmentation insights to marketing decisions

Element sequence:
  1. Hook / Bridge-in (Text)
  2. Learning Outcomes Widget
  3. Teaching Block 1 (WLO 1.1)
  4. Video: Customer Segmentation Fundamentals (3 min)
  5. Tiles: Segmentation Variables (WLO 1.1)
  6. AI Chat Widget: Customer Insights Expert
  7. Teaching Block 2 (WLO 1.2)
  8. Interactive Widget: Persona Builder Tool (WLO 1.2)
  9. Source Accordion: Segmentation Research
  10. Practice Quiz (WLO 1.1, 1.2) — 3 questions
  11. Teaching Block 3 (WLO 1.3)
  12. Interactive Widget: Segmentation Trade-offs (WLO 1.3)
  13. Go Deeper Accordion: Customer Analytics Resources
  14. Module Reflection
  15. Anchor Project Connection
  16. Transition Note

Widget specifications:
  Persona Builder Tool:
    Type: Interactive Simulator
    Purpose: Learners input customer attributes and generate persona profiles
    Inputs: Demographics (age, income), behaviors (purchase frequency, channel preference), goals
    Outputs: Persona card with targeting recommendations
    Outcome: WLO 1.2
    Status: TO BE BUILT

  Segmentation Trade-offs:
    Type: Case Study Infographic
    Purpose: Learners compare broad vs narrow segmentation strategies
    Dimensions: Reach vs relevance, cost efficiency, personalization depth
    Comparison: Mass marketing vs micro-segmentation with real brand examples
    Outcome: WLO 1.3
    Status: TO BE BUILT

AI Chat Expert:
  Name: Customer Insights Expert
  Focus areas:
    - How to choose segmentation variables for different industries
    - Balancing data-driven personas with qualitative insights
    - Common segmentation mistakes and how to avoid them
  Common questions:
    - "How many segments should I have?"
    - "What if my segments overlap?"
    - "How do I validate my personas?"
  Boundaries: Redirect technical data questions (CRM setup, data pipelines) to Week 3

Constraints:
  - Time target: 30 minutes
  - Special requirements: Video requires production

Content notes:
  - B2B and B2C examples throughout
  - Connect to Week 1 Anchor Project milestone (initial customer analysis)
  - Reference industry frameworks (RFM, behavioral segmentation, psychographics)
  - Target audience: Mid-career marketers transitioning to data-driven roles
```

# Sample 4: Complex Module with Multiple Widgets

```
HANDOFF PACK

Course format: Self-paced

Module:
  Number: 3
  Title: Pricing Strategy Fundamentals

Outcomes practiced:
  - MLO 1: Explain the relationship between price, volume, and profit margin
  - MLO 2: Calculate break-even points for different pricing scenarios
  - MLO 3: Compare cost-plus vs value-based pricing approaches
  - MLO 4: Recommend pricing adjustments based on market position

Element sequence:
  1. Hook / Bridge-in (Text)
  2. Learning Outcomes Widget
  3. Teaching Block 1 (MLO 1)
  4. Interactive Widget: Price-Volume-Profit Explorer (MLO 1)
  5. AI Chat Widget: Pricing Strategy Expert
  6. Teaching Block 2 (MLO 2)
  7. Interactive Widget: Break-Even Calculator (MLO 2)
  8. Practice Quiz 1 (MLO 1, MLO 2) — 2 questions
  9. Teaching Block 3 (MLO 3)
  10. Interactive Widget: Pricing Philosophy Comparison (MLO 3)
  11. Source Accordion: Pricing Research
  12. Teaching Block 4 (MLO 4)
  13. Interactive Widget: Market Position Analyzer (MLO 4)
  14. Practice Quiz 2 (MLO 3, MLO 4) — 2 questions
  15. Go Deeper Accordion: Advanced Pricing Resources
  16. Module Reflection
  17. Final Assessment Connection
  18. Transition Note

Widget specifications:
  Price-Volume-Profit Explorer:
    Type: Interactive Simulator
    Purpose: Learners adjust price and see impact on volume and total profit
    Inputs: Unit price ($), estimated volume, unit cost, fixed costs
    Outputs: Total revenue, total profit, profit margin (%)
    Outcome: MLO 1
    Status: TO BE BUILT

  Break-Even Calculator:
    Type: Interactive Simulator
    Purpose: Learners input costs and price to find break-even volume
    Inputs: Fixed costs, variable cost per unit, selling price per unit
    Outputs: Break-even units, break-even revenue, margin of safety
    Outcome: MLO 2
    Status: TO BE BUILT

  Pricing Philosophy Comparison:
    Type: Case Study Infographic
    Purpose: Compare cost-plus vs value-based pricing with real examples
    Dimensions: Calculation method, risk profile, market fit, industry examples
    Comparison: Manufacturing (cost-plus) vs SaaS (value-based) case studies
    Outcome: MLO 3
    Status: TO BE BUILT

  Market Position Analyzer:
    Type: Interactive Simulator
    Purpose: Learners position their product and get pricing recommendations
    Inputs: Competitor prices, perceived quality (1-10), feature comparison
    Outputs: Recommended price range, positioning map, risk assessment
    Outcome: MLO 4
    Status: TO BE BUILT

AI Chat Expert:
  Name: Pricing Strategy Expert
  Focus areas:
    - How to think about price elasticity in practice
    - When to use premium vs penetration pricing
    - Psychology of pricing (anchoring, decoy effect)
  Common questions:
    - "How do I know if I'm leaving money on the table?"
    - "Should I match competitor prices?"
    - "How do I price a new product with no market data?"
  Boundaries: Redirect legal/regulatory questions to compliance resources

Constraints:
  - Time target: 50 minutes
  - Special requirements: Pricing Philosophy Comparison uses real company data (anonymized)

Content notes:
  - Mix of B2B and B2C pricing examples
  - Include both product and service pricing scenarios
  - Target audience: Product managers and marketing leads
  - Reference: Harvard Business Review pricing frameworks
```
