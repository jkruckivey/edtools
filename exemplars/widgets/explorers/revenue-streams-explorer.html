Week 1 Foundation

The 5 Revenue Streams

Professional sports teams generate revenue from five interconnected sources. Understanding their relative size, growth potential, and stability is essential for strategic analysis.

Media Rights — 40%
Ticketing — 25%
Sponsorship — 20%
Merchandise — 10%
Betting — 5%
Media Rights 40%
Growth: 8%/yr Stability: High

TV and streaming rights. Long-term contracts provide predictable revenue.

Ticketing 25%
Growth: 5%/yr Stability: High

Season tickets, single-game, premium seating. Capacity-constrained.

Sponsorship 20%
Growth: 12%/yr Stability: Medium

Corporate partnerships, naming rights. Value tied to visibility.

Merchandise 10%
Growth: 10%/yr Stability: Variable

Jerseys, apparel. Depends on team performance and star players.

Betting/Gaming 5%
Growth: 35%/yr Stability: Low

Sports betting partnerships, fantasy sports. Fastest-growing but faces regulatory uncertainty. Smallest share today, but projected to reach 15-20% within a decade.

Key Observation

The highest-growth stream (betting at 35%) is currently the smallest, while the largest stream (media rights) grows more slowly. Growth rate alone doesn't determine strategic importance—you need to consider both current size and future trajectory.
