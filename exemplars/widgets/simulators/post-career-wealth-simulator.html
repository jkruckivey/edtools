Post-Career Wealth Simulator

Explore how career-stage decisions compound into dramatically different outcomes

What This Tool Does
  • Simulates a 20-year career arc from rookie (age 22) through retirement (age 65)
  • Models how lifestyle spending, investment strategy, and equity vs. endorsement decisions compound over time
  • Compares your path to preset scenarios (Lifestyle Trap, Conservative Builder, Magic Johnson-style)
Model Simplifications
  • Salary progressions are hypothetical: The $2M → $25M career arc is illustrative — actual salaries vary enormously by sport, position, and performance
  • Investment returns are simplified averages: "Conservative" at 4%, "Balanced" at 7%, "Aggressive" at 10% are long-term averages — actual returns are volatile year-to-year
  • Equity outcomes are speculative: The "$3M → $140M business exit" scenario represents exceptional outcomes — most athlete business investments fail or underperform
  • No taxes, divorce, or bad advice modeled: Real athlete wealth is eroded by factors not captured here — advisors, family obligations, legal issues
  • Celebrity comparisons are illustrative: "Magic Johnson-style" and "Allen Iverson-style" scenarios are dramatized patterns, not biographical accounts

This simulator illustrates how compounding and timing affect long-term wealth. The scenarios are designed to teach principles, not predict actual outcomes.
