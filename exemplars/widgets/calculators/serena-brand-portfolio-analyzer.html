Model Simplifications
  • Serena's portfolio is estimated, not verified: The $350M endorsements, $500M net worth, and Serena Ventures returns are approximated from public reporting — not audited disclosures
  • Growth rates are illustrative assumptions: Owned businesses at 25% annual growth and investments at 10% are aggressive estimates — many celebrity businesses fail or underperform
  • Endorsement decline is a pattern, not a rule: The -8% post-retirement decline is a common trajectory, but some athletes (Jordan, Federer) maintain or grow endorsement value
  • "Alternative scenarios" are dramatized: The "Michael Jordan strategy" and "100% endorsements" scenarios are simplified teaching tools, not biographical reconstructions
  • Serena Ventures IRR is estimated: The 25-35% IRR is based on reported portfolio valuations, which may not reflect actual realized returns

This analyzer illustrates how equity vs. endorsement allocation affects long-term wealth. The scenarios are designed to teach portfolio principles, not document actual financial outcomes.
