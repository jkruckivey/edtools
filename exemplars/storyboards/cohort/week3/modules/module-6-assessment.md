**Rubric Configuration:**

---

**CRITERION 1: Ethical Complexity Understanding**

**Points:** 10

**Does not meet expectations (0-3 pts):**
Treats ethical issues as black-and-white ("sportswashing is bad"). No nuance about why decisions are complex. Doesn't reference LIV Golf specifics.

**Partially meets expectations (4-6 pts):**
Shows some understanding of ethical complexity but doesn't fully develop it. May mention sportswashing without explaining the debate around its effectiveness.

**Fully meets expectations (7-10 pts):**
Clearly articulates what makes sponsorship decisions ethically complex (e.g., "LIV Golf offers attractive economics but raises concerns about legitimizing human rights abuses"). References the sportswashing debateâ€”acknowledges that evidence is mixed and reasonable people disagree.

---

**CRITERION 2: Stakeholder Trade-off Analysis**

**Points:** 8

**Does not meet expectations (0-2 pts):**
No stakeholder analysis. Treats "the public" as monolithic. Doesn't reference different perspectives.

**Partially meets expectations (3-5 pts):**
Mentions stakeholders but doesn't analyze trade-offs. May list groups without explaining how they view the issue differently.

**Fully meets expectations (6-8 pts):**
Shows how different stakeholders have different risk tolerances: "Employees (52% uncomfortable) create internal pressure; customers (38% would avoid) create market risk; shareholders may prioritize financial returns." Acknowledges tensions between stakeholder interests.

---

**CRITERION 3: Personal Framework Articulation**

**Points:** 7

**Does not meet expectations (0-2 pts):**
No clear personal position. Hedges with "it depends" without specifying on what. No reference to roleplay experience.

**Partially meets expectations (3-4 pts):**
States a position but reasoning is thin. May say "I wouldn't sponsor LIV Golf" without explaining the threshold or framework.

**Fully meets expectations (5-7 pts):**
Clear personal framework with reasoning: "My threshold: if >20% of target customers would boycott AND employees express concern, ethics outweigh economics regardless of ROI." References Mickelson roleplay decision and what it revealed about their own values.
